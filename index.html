What is Eating Disorder

Scientific understanding is the first step toward healing

Understand Eating Disorder

Eating Disorder is an eating disorder characterized by consuming unusually large amounts of food in a short period of time, often accompanied by a sense of loss of control.

Common symptoms

It may involve rapid eating, eating until uncomfortably full, eating alone out of shame, self-induced vomiting, and intense guilt afterwards.

Scientific understanding is the first step toward healing

Psychotherapy, nutritional counseling, and support groups are effective treatment options. You don't have to face this alone.

Video Story

This video combines experimental editing with linear storytelling to portray the experience of individuals with Eating Disorder. It explores one of the key triggers, body image anxiety, as a cause for binge episodes. The narrative also reveals common compensatory behaviours, such as self-induced purging, that often follow. By illustrating both the emotional buildup and the aftermath, the video aims to offer a deeper understanding of the psychological and physical toll ED can take.
